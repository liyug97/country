Guangdong dishes


   Cantonese cuisine is Cantonese cuisine. It is one of the four traditional Chinese cuisines and eight major cuisines. It originated in Lingnan. Cantonese cuisine consists of three local flavors: Guangzhou cuisine(also known as Guangfu cuisine), Chaozhou cuisine(also known as Chaoshan cuisine), and Dongjiang cuisine(also known as Hakka cuisine). Cantonese cuisine and French cuisine are equally famous around the world. The number of overseas Chinese in Guangdong accounts for 60 % of the country. Therefore, most of the Chinese restaurants in countries around the world are mainly Cantonese cuisine. Guangzhou cuisine includes the Pearl River Delta and Shaoguan, Maoming, Zhanjiang and other places. Guangzhou cuisine is a representative of Cantonese cuisine. Shunde has also been awarded the title of "Food Capital of the World" by UNESCO. Dongjiang cuisine is a faction of Hakka cuisine. It originated in Dongjiang, Guangdong Province. It is famous for casserole dishes and has a unique local flavor.
