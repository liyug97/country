Sichuan cuisine


  Sichuan cuisine is Sichuan cuisine. It is one of the four traditional cuisines with Chinese characteristics, one of the eight major cuisines in China, and a master of Chinese cuisine. Modern Sichuan cuisine emerged in the Qing Dynasty and the Republic of China, and was innovated and developed after the founding of New China. Sichuan cuisine is dominated by home-cooked dishes, supplemented by high-end dishes. Most of the ingredients are daily snacks, and there are also many rare seafood. Its characteristics are that red taste emphasizes hemp, spicy, fresh and fragrant; The White taste is changeable, including sweet, fragrant, strange taste and many other flavors. Representative dishes include Kung Pao Chicken, husband and wife lung slices, pot meat, and Dongpo meat. Other classic dishes include: Lollipop chicken,Sichuan hot pot, chestnut roast chicken, spicy chicken and so on.
