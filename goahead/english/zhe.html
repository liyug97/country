Zhejiang cuisine


    Zhejiang cuisine, referred to as Zhejiang cuisine, is one of the eight traditional Chinese cuisines. Its scenery is beautiful and its products are rich. Therefore, it is said: "There is heaven and there is Suhang." Located on the coast of China's East China Sea, Zhejiang Province is known as the land of fish and rice. The hills in the Southwest are undulating and rich in wild game. The eastern coastal fisheries with rich aquatic resources. There are more than 500 kinds of economic fish and shell aquatic products. The total output value in the country, and the products are rich. The delicacies are beautiful, unique and famous. Zhejiang cuisine has a long history. Zhejiang cuisine is based on four major schools. As a whole, it has a relatively obvious characteristic style and has four common characteristics: exquisite selection, unique cooking, emphasis on this taste, and fine production.
