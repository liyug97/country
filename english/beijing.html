Forbidden City

The Forbidden City in Beijing is the royal palace of the Ming and Qing Dynasties in China. It was formerly known as the Forbidden City. It is located in the center of Beijing's central axis and is the essence of ancient Chinese palace architecture. The Forbidden City in Beijing is centered on the three main halls. It covers an area of 720,000 square meters and has a construction area of about 150,000 square meters. There are more than 70 large and small palaces and more than 9,000 houses. It is the largest and best-preserved Beijing Forbidden City in the world. It was built in the 4th year of Ming Chengzu Yongle (1406), built on the Forbidden City of Nanjing, and built in the 18th year of Yongle (1420). It is a rectangular city, 961 meters long from north to south, 753 meters wide from east to west, with a wall of 10 meters high on all sides and a moat with a width of 52 meters outside the city. The buildings in the Forbidden City are divided into two parts: the outer dynasty and the inner court. The center of the outer dynasty is the Taihe Temple, the Zhonghe Temple, and the Baohe Temple. They are collectively called the Three Great Halls and are the place where the state holds a grand ceremony. The center of the inner court is the Qianqing Palace, the Jiaotai Hall, and the Kunning Palace. They are collectively called the Housan Palace. They are the palaces where the emperors and empresses live.

Known as the world's five major palaces (Versailles, France, Buckingham Palace, White House, Russian Kremlin), the Forbidden City is the national AAAAA tourist attraction. It was listed as the first national key cultural relics protection unit in 1961; Listed as a World Heritage Site.

From January 2012 to June 2018, the National Palace Museum received a total of 100 million visitors. Starting in 2019, the National Palace Museum will try to sell tickets in separate time slots. On September 3, 2018, the Palace of the Yangxin Palace officially entered the implementation stage of the research and restoration of ancient buildings. On March 4, 2019, the National Palace Museum announced the exhibition plan for the second half of 2019.

Museum role

The founding of the Palace Museum has two meanings: one is the victory of the democratic revolution, a fatal blow to the restoration forces; the other is a great achievement in the history of Chinese culture and art. In the 14th year of the Republic of China (1925), the inauguration ceremony of the Palace Museum was held on October 10. The museum is an institution that collects, researches, displays and preserves objects for the purpose of cultural education. It will become a symbol of the monarchy and a precious cultural relic that can only be enjoyed by the emperor and become a common wealth of the whole nation. When the Palace Museum was established, it formulated the "Provisions of the Provisional Council of the Palace Museum." In the 17th year of the Republic of China (1928), the National Government promulgated the "Organization Law of the Palace Museum", which was the first law on museums in Chinese history, and later promulgated the "Regulations of the Palace Museum of the Republic of China." These two documents are of great significance in the history of the development of the Palace Museum, marking the beginning of the museum's maturity and the beginning of the Chinese museum's career.
