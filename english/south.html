South-to-North Water Transfer

“South-to-North Water Transfer Project” is the “South-to-North Water Transfer Project”. It is a strategic project of the People’s Republic of China. It is divided into three lines: east, middle and west. The starting point of the east line project is located in Jiangdu Water Conservancy Project in Yangzhou, Jiangsu Province. The starting point of the middle line project is located in the Danjiangkou Reservoir in the middle and upper reaches of the Han River. It is the four provinces (cities) of Henan, Hebei, Beijing and Tianjin.

The concept of the project was initiated in 1952 when Chairman Mao Zedong inspected the Yellow River. Since then, after more than 50 programs have been analyzed and compared, the water transfer program has obtained a large number of valuable results. The South-to-North Water Transfer Project Planning Area covers a population of 438 million people and a water transfer scale of 44.8 billion cubic meters. The total length of the east, middle and west trunk lines of the project is 4,350 kilometers. The total length of the first-phase project of the East and Central Lines is 2,899 kilometers, and the supporting branch of the first-grade and six-provincial branches along the line is about 2,700 kilometers.

In September 2012, the resettlement of the Danjiangkou reservoir area in the Middle Route of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project was fully completed. The South-to-North Water Diversion Project mainly solves the problem of water shortage in the northern part of China, especially the Huang-Huai-Hai Basin. The planned population is 438 million. There are three water transfer lines on the east, middle and west lines. Through the connection of the three water transfer lines with the Yangtze, Yellow, Huaihe and Haihe rivers, the overall layout of the “four horizontal and three vertical” is the main layout to facilitate the realization of China. The rational allocation of water resources from north to south and between east and west.

The Middle Route of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project and the East Route of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project (Phase I) have been completed and transferred to the northern region. As of now, the West Line project is still in the planning stage and has not started construction.

1Historical evolution

Project proposal

In 1952, when Comrade Mao Zedong inspected the Yellow River, he proposed: "There is more water in the south and less water in the north. If possible, it is possible to borrow some water." This is also the first time that the grand idea of ​​the South-to-North Water Diversion was proposed.

On February 16, 1953, Mao Zedong went to Wuhan for the first time after the founding of New China. He inspected the banks of the Han River and visited the East Lake by wooden boat. On the "Yangtze River" ship, Mao Zedong asked Lin Yishan: "There is more water in the south, less water in the north, can you borrow some water to the north?" When the ship arrived in Nanjing, Mao Zedong said to Lin Yishan: The Three Gorges problem is temporarily not considered, I am only the first Touch the bottom, but the work of the South-to-North Water Transfer should be tightened.

In August 1958, the "Directive of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on Water Conservancy Work" was promulgated, and the South-North Water Transfer was officially proposed for the first time.

In 1959, the Chinese Academy of Sciences and the Ministry of Water and Electricity held a "Working Conference on the Study of the South-to-North Water Diversion in the Western Region" in Beijing. The guidelines for the South-to-North Water Diversion Project were: "Also-adjust and apply, comprehensively utilize, make overall plans, and benefit both the North and the South. To make more water, to make the best use of water, and to make the best use of it."

In 1979, the "Government Work Report" adopted by the Fifth Session of the National People's Congress officially proposed: "Building a project to channel the Yangtze River to the South-to-North Water Transfer Project north of the Yellow River."

Project Research Theory

In December 1979, the Ministry of Water Resources formally established the South-to-North Water Transfer Planning Office to coordinate and coordinate the work of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project across the country.

In July 1987, the State Planning Commission officially issued a notice and decided to include the West Route Project of South-to-North Water Transfer in the “Seventh Five-Year Plan”.

In April 1991, the Fourth Session of the Seventh National People's Congress included the "South-to-North Water Transfer" in the "Eighth Five-Year Plan" and the Ten-Year Plan.

In October 1992, the 14th National Congress of the Communist Party of China included the "South-to-North Water Transfer" as one of China's cross-century backbone projects.

In December 1995, the South-to-North Water Transfer Project began a comprehensive demonstration.

On June 5, 2000, the planning of the South-to-North Water Diversion Project was carried out in an orderly manner. After several decades of research, the overall pattern of the South-to-North Water Diversion Project was designated as the west, middle and east lines, and water was transferred from the upper, middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River Basin.

Project approval period

On October 10, 2002, the Standing Committee of the Political Bureau of the CPC Central Committee reviewed and approved the "Master Plan for the South-to-North Water Transfer Project" approved by the State Council.

On December 23, 2002, the State Council officially approved the "South-to-North Water Transfer Master Plan."

  

Project construction period

On December 27, 2002, the South-to-North Water Transfer Project officially started. The Jiangsu Sanchabao Project and the Shandong Section Jiping Main Canal Project became the first batch of construction projects for the East Route of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project.

On December 30, 2003, the emergency water supply project for the Beijing-Shijiazhuang section of the Middle Route of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project started, marking the official start of the first phase of the South-North Water Transfer Middle Line.

On September 26, 2005, the landmark project of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project and the control project of the Danjiangkou Reservoir in the mid-line water source, the Danjiangkou Dam Heightening Project, officially started, marking the entry into the full implementation phase of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project.

On September 28, 2008, the emergency water supply project of the Beijing-Shijiazhuang section of the Middle Route of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project was completed.

On November 25, 2008, Hubei Province held a mobilization meeting for the pilot project of the Danjiangkou reservoir area in Wuhan, marking the full launch of the pilot project for the Danjiangkou reservoir area in the middle line of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project.

On February 26, 2009, the construction of the Xinglong Water Conservancy Project of the Middle Route of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project started, marking the start of the construction of seven provinces and cities in the East and Central Lines of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project.

On March 31, 2010, the 54 dam sections of the Danjiangkou dam were all raised to the top, marking a major stage victory for the Danjiangkou dam heightening project.

In September 2012, the resettlement of the Danjiangkou Reservoir in the middle line of the South-to-North Water Transfer Project was fully completed.

The total length of the main line of the Central Line and the Tianjin Main Canal are 1432 kilometers. The geographical climate varies greatly along the route. There is a problem of winter channel freezing in the north section of Anyang. After the main channel is frozen, the water delivery capacity will naturally drop, affecting the water delivery. In addition, if the main canal is not properly operated during the ice age, ice congestion and ice dam accidents may occur, which threatens the safety of the channel.

A netizen posted a message saying that "the north is too cold, and the water is frozen into ice."
