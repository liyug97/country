Wanli Great Wall

  

The Great Wall, also known as the Great Wall It is a military defense project in ancient China. It is a tall, sturdy and continuous long raft that is used to limit enemy riding. The Great Wall is not a purely isolated city wall, but a defense system that combines a city wall as the main body and a large number of cities, barriers, pavilions, and standards.

The history of the construction of the Great Wall can be traced back to the Western Zhou Dynasty. The famous allusion of the martyrdom of the capital, Xi'an (now Xi'an, Shaanxi Province) originated here. During the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period, the countries competed for hegemony and defended each other. The Great Wall built the first climax, but the length of construction was relatively short. After the Qin and the Six Kingdoms unified the world, Qin Shihuang connected and repaired the Great Wall of the Warring States, and was known as the Great Wall. The Ming Dynasty was the last dynasty to overhaul the Great Wall. Today, many of the Great Walls that people saw were built at this time.

The Great Wall resources are mainly distributed in 15 provinces, autonomous regions, such as Hebei, Beijing, Tianjin, Shanxi, Shaanxi, Gansu, Inner Mongolia, Heilongjiang, Jilin, Liaoning, Shandong, Henan, Qinghai, Ningxia and Xinjiang. Among them, Shaanxi Province is the province with the richest resources in China's Great Wall. The length of the Great Wall in China is 1,838 kilometers. According to the results of the National Great Wall Resources Survey of the Cultural Relics and Surveying and Mapping Department, the total length of the Ming Great Wall is 8851.8 kilometers, and the Qin and Han Great Walls exceed 10,000 kilometers, with a total length of more than 21,000 kilometers.

1History

  

Overview

The Great Wall is an ancient defense project with the longest construction time and the largest amount of engineering in China. Since the Western Zhou Dynasty, it has been continuously built for more than 2,000 years and distributed on vast land in northern and central China. More than 50,000 kilometers.

Since Qin Shihuang, all the dynasties that ruled the Central Plains region had to build the Great Wall. More than ten dynasties, including Han, Jin, Northern Wei, Eastern Wei, Western Wei, Northern Qi, Northern Zhou, Sui, Tang, Song, Liao, Jin, Yuan, Ming and Qing, have built the Great Wall on different scales. From the perspective of the ruling nation building the Great Wall, in addition to the Han nationality, many dynasties that ruled China by the minority also repaired the Great Wall, and there were more dynasties than the Han dynasty. During the Kangxi period of the Qing Dynasty, although the construction of the Great Wall was stopped, the Great Wall was built in some places. It can be said that since the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period, it has not stopped building for more than 2,000 years in the Qing Dynasty.

According to historical documents, more than 20 vassal states and feudal dynasties built the Great Wall. If the Great Wall built in each era is added up, there are more than 100,000 miles, including the length of the Great Wall of the Qin, Han and Ming dynasties. More than 10,000 miles.

Pre-Qin

The history of the Great Wall construction can be traced back to the Western Zhou Dynasty. In order to defend against the attacks of the northern nomadic people, the Zhou Dynasty built a series of castles "Liecheng" for defense. In the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period, the countries in order to fight for hegemony and defend each other, built the Great Wall on the border according to their respective defensive needs. The earliest building was the "Chu Fang City" in the 7th century BC, followed by Qi, Han, Wei, Zhao and Yan. The vassal states of the Qin and Qin dynasties have successively built the "Great Walls of the Privileges and Defences" for self-defense. Among them, the Qin, Zhao, and Yan countries and the powerful nomadic Xiongnu in the north are adjacent to each other. At the same time, they built the "Great Wall of the Rejection of the Hu" in the north, and the monarchs of the past dynasties almost all strengthened and improved. At this time, the Great Wall is characterized by different directions in the east, south, west and north, and the length is short, ranging from several hundred kilometers to 1000-2000 kilometers. In order to distinguish it from the Great Wall of the Great Emperor of Qin Shihuang, the historian called it the "pre-Qin Great Wall."

  

Qin Dynasty

Twenty-six years of Qin Shihuang (221 BC), Qin annexed the six countries and unified the world, establishing the first unified multi-ethnic and centralized state in Chinese history. In order to maintain and consolidate the security of the unprecedented unified empire, Qin Shihuang successively adopted a series of major strategic measures for national defense construction and defensive preparation. One of them was the large-scale construction of the Great Wall. In the 32nd year of Emperor Qin Shihuang (the first 215 years), the generals rate 300,000 troops to attack the Xiongnu, take the Henan area, and then build up "Linqi in the west (now Shanni County, Gansu), east to Liaodong (now Liaoning Province), The Great Wall of more than 10,000 miles. Since Qin Shihuang built the Great Wall, it has been known as the Great Wall.

The Great Wall of Qin Shihuang is roughly: West from Ganyu County, Gansu Province, north of the Luan River to Linyi County, from Linyi County to the northeast of Dingxi County to the northeast to Guyuan County of Ningxia. From Guyuan to the northeast through the ring county of Gansu Province, Shaanxi Province, Jingbian, Hengshan, Yulin, Shenmu, and then north to the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region in Tokto South, to the south bank of the Yellow River. The Great Wall to the north of the Yellow River is from the Langshan Mountain in the western part of the Yinshan Mountain Range. It is inserted into the north of the Daqing Mountain in the east and continues to the east through the Inner Mongolia Jining and Xinghe to the Shangyi County in Hebei. From Shangyi to the northeast through Zhangbei and Weichang counties in Hebei Province, and then eastward through Fushun and Benxi to the southeast, ending at the Qingchuan River in the northwest of Pyongyang, North Korea.

In the early Han Dynasty, the Xiongnu took the Huaxia War and crossed the Qin Dynasty to build the Great Wall, and the Han Empire confronted the Qin, Zhao and Yan Great Walls of the Warring States. Because the Great Wall was in disrepair for a long time, the northern defenders were scarce, so the powerful Huns continued to plunder within the Great Wall. After the emperor of Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty, he successively launched several wars against the Xiongnu, expelled him to Mobei, repaired the Qin Great Wall built by Mengzi, and built the Great Wall, and built a Dawanyu Town in the west. The Great Wall, which is nearly 10,000 kilometers long on the north bank of the Yalu River. At this time, the Qin, Zhao, and Yan Great Walls of the Warring States Period were abandoned and their military defenses ended.

The Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty built the Great Wall to expel the Huns. It played a great role in maintaining the security of the Western Han Empire. It also safeguarded the smooth and safe Silk Road and had extraordinary historical significance.

beacon fire

The Great Wall system has a large number of beacon fire (Beacon) as the information delivery system, which is the oldest but effective way of messaging.

The ancient border defense alarm has two kinds of signals. In case of enemy situation, the smoke during the day is called “beacon”, and the fire at night is called “燧”, the platform is connected, and the message is transmitted. Smoke is burned during the day and fire is raised at night because the sun is strong during the day, the fire is hard to see, the smoke is relatively eye-catching, and the smoke at night is not obvious, and the fire can be seen from a long distance. This is a very scientific method. In order to report how many enemy soldiers committed, it is also distinguished by the number of smokers and fires. By the time the Ming Dynasty was still burning cigarettes and raising the number of fires, the sound of guns was added to enhance the effect of the alarm, so that the military situation could be quickly transmitted thousands of miles away. According to the Ming Dynasty system, a smoke and a gun show that the enemy is about 100 people; two smokers and two guns, about 500 enemy troops; more than 1,000 people to raise three smoke and three guns. According to the location and function of the configuration, the beacon fire can be divided into four groups: the beacon tower located in the depths of the desert is the front line for issuing warnings. The beacons set along the two sides of the Great Wall transmit information along the line, and the series from the Great Wall to the Jingshi The beacon tower is in contact with the dynasty's central government, and a group of beacon towers are in contact with local governments and garrisons near the Great Wall.

The building of the plaque was earlier than the Great Wall, but since the Great Wall appeared, the beacon tower along the Great Wall was closely integrated with the Great Wall and became an important part of the Great Wall defense system, and some were built on the Great Wall. The beacons are generally about 10 miles apart, and the Ming Dynasty also has a distance of about 5 miles.

The layout of beacon fire is also very important. The key is to place it in the place where the mountain is dangerous or the peak and the turn, and the three cockroaches that must be adjacent are in the field of view of each other, so that the message can be viewed and transmitted at any time.
