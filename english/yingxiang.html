Character influence

Literary influence

Lu Xun is a cultural giant in the 20th century. he has made great contributions in many fields such as novels, essays, essays, woodcuts, modern poetry, archaic poetry, translation of famous works, collation of ancient books and modern scholarship. As the great founder of modern Chinese literature, the few novels created by Lu Xun have established new forms of Chinese novels. His prose "show that achievements of the literary revolution"; His essays are full of modernity, freedom, criticism and fighting, and are the "critical weapons" most frequently used by later writers. His essays are also encyclopedias of Chinese society, politics, history, law, religion, morality, philosophy, literature, art and even cultural psychology, people's nature, people's feelings and folk customs. Almost all Chinese writers have developed different literary styles and styles on the basis of Lu Xun's creation. As a translator, he has translated a large number of foreign literary works and scientific and natural works, which have made great contributions to opening up people's wisdom and introducing advanced scientific and cultural thoughts. As an art lover, Lu Xun introduced a large number of western woodcut and printmaking works and supported young people to learn woodcut and printmaking art in spirit, theory and spirit, which greatly promoted the spread and development of modern woodcut and printmaking in modern China and made outstanding contributions to the fine arts cause in modern China.

academic impact

as an academic researcher, on the one hand, Lu Xun used western literary concepts to study Chinese classical novels and wrote "a brief history of Chinese novels", which not only ended the era when "Chinese novels came from nowhere", but also created a typical example of literary history works, "offering countless methods for later studies". Guo Moruo regarded Wang Guowei's "the history of song and yuan operas" and Guo Guo Guowei's "the history of song and yuan operas" as a double wall in modern Chinese academic history. On the other hand, he collated, proofread and collated dozens of ancient books, such as "Ancient Novels Hook and Sink", "Ji Kangji", "Han Portrait Collection" and "Miscellaneous Collection of Historical Books in Huiji County", which left a huge fortune for later classical literature research.

spiritual impact

Lu Xun can be called the national soul of modern China. his spirit has deeply influenced his readers, researchers, and even generations of modern Chinese writers and intellectuals. Lu Xun is also one of the world cultural giants in the 20th century. He created the literature of "both inside and outside, merging with the world's trend of thought of the times, but not undermining China's national character" and having a unique personal style of "Chinese people who want to participate in the world's undertakings today". He, like the world's outstanding thinkers and writers of the same period, paid close attention to the common problems faced by human beings while paying attention to his own nation, and made his own unique contribution.

political influence

Lu Xun pursued democracy all his life. as early as the beginning of the new literature movement, Lu Xun declared war on the old feudal culture and constantly fought against the old ideology and culture that oppressed the people. After the "Female Normal University Student Movement" took place, Lu Xun argued with the warlords persecuting the students. Lu Xun defying violence and writing a war showed the spirit of an upright scholar. Then the Kuomintang boldly disclosed its dark rule under its rule. In the thirties, Lu Xun fought against the wrong left-leaning line of literature and art in the Communist Party. Later, he established a left-wing writers' alliance led by proletarian literature and art ideas in cooperation with literary and art workers under the leadership of the Party. Lu Xun, as the leader of the left-wing alliance, led the left-wing alliance to smash the Kuomintang reactionary literature and art's plot to strangle proletarian literature and art.
