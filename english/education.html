2History


  

Europe

  

In the West, the origins of education are greatly influenced by religious organizations: priests recognize the importance of education and establish a school system. In Europe, many of the original universities have a Catholic background. A new National Scottish Church was established after the Scottish Reformation.

  

China

  

Chinese education begins in Chinese classical writings, not religious organizations. In the early days, Chinese officials appointed education officials to manage the empire. The civil service examination system used to select officials was established in the Sui Dynasty. Tang Taizong of the Tang Dynasty perfected the imperial examination system.

  

At the end of the 19th century, He Ziyuan and Qiu Fengjia and other sages first established a new school such as Yunandong Primary School, Tongren School, Xingmin Middle School, and Shima Primary School. They cultivated the 1911 Revolution and the national construction. A large number of valuable talents. Subsequently, China's modern education system has been continuously improved and strengthened.

  

In the 1990s, China’s education system has undergone major changes, and private capital has re-entered the education field.

Japan

  

The origin of Japanese education is closely related to religion. The school is a temple for young people who want to study Buddhism as a monk. Later, the children who want to learn are going to "Tera-koya" (meaning temple lodge) to learn how to read and write Japanese.

India

  

Institutionalized education in India has a long history, and it is verified that India has a well-developed education system before the rise of Buddhism. The modernization of education can be divided into two major stages of development during the colonial period and after independence. The colonial period was the starting stage of India's educational modernization, including the transition period from traditional education to modern education (1498-19th century); the formation period of the colonial Indian education system, the westernization period of Indian education (early 19th century to 20th century) The first period, the rise and development of national education (in the early 20th century ~ 1947 independence) three periods. After independence in 1947, Indian education entered its all-round development stage.

World Education Trends in the 21st Century

  

The percentage of illiterate people who have not received any school education has declined over the past few decades. From 36% in 1960 to 25% in 2000.

  

In developing countries, the percentage of illiterate people in 2000 was only half of that in 1970. In developed countries, illiteracy has also fallen from 6% to 1%, and those who have not received school education have fallen from 5% to 2%.

  

On December 3, 2018, the 44th plenary meeting of the 73rd session of the UN General Assembly was held at the United Nations Headquarters in New York. The meeting adopted a resolution to set January 24th as the International Education Day.
